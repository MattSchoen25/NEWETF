What are the Advantages of a Buffered ETF?

Targeted Outcomes

Buffered ETFs provide market access to targeted outcome investments in a single product eliminating the need to execute multi leg derivative contracts that compose a defined outcome investment objective strategy.

 Market Exposure with Buffer

Buffered ETFs give investors market exposure with defined downside buffer levels over a specified time period which limits some of the uncertainty associated with traditional equity investing.

 Reduced Risk

The Buffered ETF is a Fund and is subject to all investment, governance and structural rules under the 1940 Act regulated by the SEC. As you owner of the fund, the investor has an undivided interest in the underlying assets of the fund. This distinction reduces the credit risk from exchange-traded notes (ETNs) as (ETNs) do not own underlying assets, but rather debt offering of the issuer (Generally a Bank). 

 Low Cost Strategy Execution

Low Cost and Liquid execution: Below market fees for execution of exchange-traded investment vehicles in a liquid, open, and transparent market.
